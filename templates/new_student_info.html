Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost of living for Columbia, Missouri, USA?

    As of 3/22/2020, according to the first website returned when doing a google search for cost of living in Columbia, Missouri, USA, the average cost of living index is 87.9. The website uses 100 to denote the average cost of living in the USA. Therefore the average cost of living for Columbia, Missouri, USA is below average. (Of course the data will vary from year to year and different websites might calculate differently.)

    On the topic of living in Columbia, Missouri, USA:

    • Columbia, Missouri, USA is frequently listed as one of the top most livable college towns in the USA. For instance according to an article in USA Today (dated 9/2019) Columbia, Missouri is ranked #17 among all college towns in the USA. Of course, rankings vary year to year and vary website to website. For instance livability.com ranks Columbia, Missouri as #7 in 2015.
    • According to a report by ASecureLife.com , Columbia College is No. 3 most secure college campus in the United States. The publication ranked campuses based on property crime rates after analyzing 10 years' worth of crime data from hundreds of public and nonprofit colleges.

Do I need to know how to program to get into your CS program?

    No. In our computer science program, we do not assume you have prior programming experience. We have very successful graduates who enter our computer science program without any programming background.

Portal Into Computer Science

What is PiCS?

    PiCS (or Portal into Computer Science) is the Computer Science outreach program of Columbia College.

Goal of PiCS

    The goal of PiCS is to introduce K-12 students to the exciting area of Computer Science. We have visited Benton Elementary School and Alpha Hart Lewis Elementary School to talk about computer science and to teach basic programming.

    In spring 2017 we organized an “Hour of Code” event for Jeff Middle School. We have organized several high school events including an annual High School Programming Contest since spring 2015. We are also organizing several summer camps for middle school and high school students.

    More programs for elementary school students are forthcoming.
