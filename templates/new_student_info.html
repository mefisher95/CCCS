Overview

Our department hosts a thriving community of Computer Scientists from Nepal, many of which have gone on to work for top companies, found their own start ups, and continue on to graduate school to pursue their PhDs.

Columbia College offers full-tuition scholarships to international students. Besides that, the computer science program also provides opportunity for on-campus work-study employment.

To be successful in obtaining the full-tuition scholarship, you must be strong academically and prove that you are someone who will contribute positively to the computer science rogram and also to student life in Columbia College. Besides being a top student academically, a Columbia College scholar is also someone who is active in the college. Therefore it is important for you to show that you are active in your student clubs or organizations at school and/or have some service record such as volunteer work in your community. We are not just looking for someone who is academically strong. We are looking for someone who is concerned about the world and whose goal is to make the world a better place.

Steps to Take

    Take SAT and TOEFL after your final exams

    • SAT requires 2 months preparation and TOEFL requires 1 month of preparation
    • Try to register for the SAT and TOEFL as early as possible. There are limited exam centers in Nepal and they may run out of seats.
    • The ideal time to take the SAT is Oct-Nov and TOEFL on May-June so that you can take it again in December if your score was not satisfactory.
    • Khan Academy has a lot of free resources to prepare for the SAT.
    • Practice and early preperation is key

    Create a common application account and fill out the details

    • Link: commonapp

    Ask your teachers for the letter of recommendation

    • A Letter of Recommendation is a document where your instructor describes your skills and potential
    • It is good to have 3 letters of recommendation
        Usually, universities asks for 2 letters of recommendation

    Draft your Personal Essay

    • Start thinking about the personal essay early
    • There are multiple personal essays to choose from, in common application
    • You should answer one of them in an elegant way
    • Start brainstorming early, consult your English teacher, and try to come up with an essay that describes you well

    Apply

    • Begin after you are done with these processes and SAT/ACT and TOEFL/IELTS

Scholarship Interview

Prepare for The Interview

    One very important advice
    • For overseas/international students, the scholarship interview is done through a video chat (probably either skype or zoom)
    • It is important to speak clearly and slowly
    • You are under the mercy of internet bandwidth
    We once had a strong candidate who did not do well during the interview process because of bad internet connection. And because he was talking too fast, some of the spoken words were either garbled or totally lost in transmission. So practice talking slowly with a friend doing a mock interview.

Get Advice

  • You might want to google for advice and common questions asked during a scholarship interview
  • You might want to ask someone to do a mock interview with you, record the session, and play it back to yourself

Be relaxed, Be Polite, and Ask Questions

  • Go into the interview relaxed. Try to calm yourself down and avoid being tense and stiff
  • Treat the interviewer with respect and courtesy, they are more likely to report back kindly on someone who treated them nicely
  • If you do not understand the question (either because the question was vague or because of bad internet connection), it is OK to ask the interviewer to repeat the question or ask for clarification

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost of living for Columbia, Missouri, USA?

    As of 3/22/2020, according to the first website returned when doing a google search for cost of living in Columbia, Missouri, USA, the average cost of living index is 87.9. The website uses 100 to denote the average cost of living in the USA. Therefore the average cost of living for Columbia, Missouri, USA is below average. (Of course the data will vary from year to year and different websites might calculate differently.)

    On the topic of living in Columbia, Missouri, USA:

    • Columbia, Missouri, USA is frequently listed as one of the top most livable college towns in the USA. For instance according to an article in USA Today (dated 9/2019) Columbia, Missouri is ranked #17 among all college towns in the USA. Of course, rankings vary year to year and vary website to website. For instance livability.com ranks Columbia, Missouri as #7 in 2015.
    • According to a report by ASecureLife.com , Columbia College is No. 3 most secure college campus in the United States. The publication ranked campuses based on property crime rates after analyzing 10 years' worth of crime data from hundreds of public and nonprofit colleges.

Do I need to know how to program to get into your CS program?

    No. In our computer science program, we do not assume you have prior programming experience. We have very successful graduates who enter our computer science program without any programming background.

Portal Into Computer Science

What is PiCS?

    PiCS (or Portal into Computer Science) is the Computer Science outreach program of Columbia College.

Goal of PiCS

    The goal of PiCS is to introduce K-12 students to the exciting area of Computer Science. We have visited Benton Elementary School and Alpha Hart Lewis Elementary School to talk about computer science and to teach basic programming.

    In spring 2017 we organized an “Hour of Code” event for Jeff Middle School. We have organized several high school events including an annual High School Programming Contest since spring 2015. We are also organizing several summer camps for middle school and high school students.

    More programs for elementary school students are forthcoming.
